📚 WHAT I LEARNED
OS security involves protecting the operating system from unauthorised access, misuse, and exploitation

User accounts and the principle of least privilege limit what each user can do — reducing the impact of a compromise

Patch management keeps the OS updated to fix known vulnerabilities before attackers can exploit them

Antivirus and host-based firewalls add layers of defence directly on the device

Auditing and logging track user activity and system events — essential for detecting and investigating incidents

🔐 WHY IT MATTERS
The OS is the foundation of every system — if it is compromised, everything running on top of it is at risk

Most cyberattacks — ransomware, privilege escalation, lateral movement — target weaknesses at the OS level
